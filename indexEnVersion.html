Introduction

Team Profile

Extreme Scale Network Computing and Service (ESNCS) research team is currently formed by professor Yong Zhao, associate professor Quan Wen、Luping Ji、Wenhong Tian、Ruini Xue and Juan Chen. With an aim to lead research in extreme scale computing and service, led by professors with overseas research and work experience, the team is planning to lead young teachers, doctorial and master students to do research in related areas.

Learnt advanced overseas teaching, research and management experiences, the team aims at cultivating a research group with considerable research capabilities and developing systems and products that can manage and schedule extreme scale size of network computing resources and store, process massive data (PB, EB and even larger).

Our Team actively strengthens international exchanges and cooperation and closely tracks the dynamics and related fields of international research, striving for the international leadership. Focus of our team is to manage and schedule large scale network resources, including large-scale clusters, grids, cloud platforms and supercomputers to conduct large-scale computing, storage, data processing and mining, and provide services on this basis.

The main research interests include resource management and scheduling of cloud data centers, task scheduling in large-scale processing, massive data processing and data mining and massive distributed storage systems.


Feature

  • All the teachers of this team have studied in the U.S., Canada for many years, familiar with international research trends of industry and research and development processes of abroad.

  • With experiences of many years of large-scale system design or large-scale data processing, team members’ research areas are highly complementary.

  • Close contacts and cooperation with internationality.

Areas

  • Scheduling and management of cloud data center resources
    • Tens of thousands or even more computer resources and corresponding network, storage devices
    • Flexible scheduling, reasonable utilization of resources, reliability and availability of the cloud service, energy saving
  • Handling and scheduling of large-scale tasks
    • Hundreds of thousands of computing nodes
    • Distribution efficiency, data correlation
  • Massive data processing and data mining
    • Web logs, user behavior, published user information
    • Storage, indexing structure, tasks and data coupling
    • Video and image
  • Massive distributed storage systems
    • Traditional database is powerless
    • Storage, indexing, backup, processing
    • Structure, scalability, consistency

    Platform

    We have several IBM blade servers, several Dell professional servers and relevant cloud management software, dozens of desktop machines. Our team is working with businesses to build cloud storage platform that has 100 nodes (more than four local area network). Our team has built several parallel processing platform，including VMware virtual machines and clusters, Hadoop cluster, Microsoft high-performance computing cluster HPC，etc. We have initially completed some application platforms, such as massive cloud task scheduler, cloud simulation system, cloud resource sharing systems and RFID middleware, and so on.
